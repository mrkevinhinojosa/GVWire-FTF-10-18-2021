SE FRESNO CAMPUS OPENING COULD HOBBLE CANNABIS RETAILER

Fresno Farms received one of 21 provisional permits to open a cannabis store, but it might be short-lived. The store’s site on Ventura Avenue is within 800 yards of Fresno Unified’s new Francine and Murray Farber Educational Center that’s scheduled to open in 2023. Fresno Farms officials thought only adults would be attending classes there.

SOCIAL WORKERS DECRY DISMAL CONDITIONS FOR KIDS HELD BY CPS

Children who have been removed from their parents custody and are awaiting placement by Child Protective Services are held in “nightmarish” spaces in downtown Fresno, I rooms littered with broken glass while children sleep on mats on conference tables and floors, surrounded by water bottles filled with urine. The conditions were highlighted Thursday by social workers represented by SEIU Local 521.

FRESNO SCHOOL BOARD MEETINGS MARKED BY DISRUPTIONS

Trustee Keshia Thomas reads a “civility warning” at the start of every Fresno Unified School Board meeting, but it appears that audience members as well as trustees either aren’t hearing it or are choosing to ignore it. Last week’s board meeting was marked by multiple outbursts.

Reedley Councilwoman Jewel Hurtado got some good news last week —a recall effort failed to collect enough signatures to get the measure on a special election ballot next year. Hurtado still faces three traffic violations and pleaded not guilty Friday through her attorney.

MAYOR SAYS TOWER THEATRE PROTESTS ‘NOT HEALTHY’ FOR FRESNO

Proponents and opponents of the Tower Theatre sale to the evangelical Christian Adventure Church have gathered for nearly a year on corners near the theater, forcing Fresno police to be a constant presence to maintain order.
